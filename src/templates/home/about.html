About CSai

CSai is a web application that allows users to ask questions to an "AI tutor" and receive answers in a conversational manner. The app is finely-tuned towards computer science related questions, while filtering out most off-topic questions. It reliably provides concise and valuable feedback as if you were bouncing questions off of a human professor or mentor. CSai is written in Python, built using the Flask framework, and leverages the power of the OpenAI API. The application is currently in beta and is not available to the public.

"Breakthroughs in A.I. are increasingly improving its capabilities—opening new and exciting avenues of education."

As a computer science student, I often found it to be a challenge to quickly get answers when I was studying a programming concept. As a college student, I would try to email the professor, and after what felt like ages, finally receive a response that was either too vague or not what I needed. Sometimes I would try posting to one of the well-known "stack" forums and receive replies written by someone that somehow assumed I was a senior engineer with years of experience. Finding help sometimes felt like I was trying to track a snow leopard in the desert. It was harder than trying to center a div in CSS! And for self-taught programmers, getting help can be even harder. Like all lazy engineers, I wanted to make the problem go away and make life easier. I wanted to build a tool for myself, and for others like me, to receive answers to programming questions, quickly and easily. Thus, CSai was born.

As technology continues to become more ingrained into our daily lives, Computer Science educators are only going to become more sought-after. However, breakthroughs in A.I. are increasingly improving its capabilities, opening new and exciting avenues of education.
